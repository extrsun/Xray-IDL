Estimate of basic parameters of galaxies


Calculation of the K-band luminosity L_K and its conversion to the mass M of a galaxy (from Zhiyuan):

The most convenient look-up for delta_nu/nu at a given band is probably on Dan
McIntosh's website: http://www.astro.umass.edu/~dmac/mags.html, in which
delta_nu/nu = 0.23 for K band.

Below are the steps to convert the 2MASS K-band magnitude to the luminosity L_K
and stellar mass M_K.

1. Look up the apparent magnitude m of the target galaxy from NED, with the
value of either K_20 (within 20 mag/arcsec^2 isophote) or K_total (total
magnitude extroplated by a Sersic fit to the intensity profile, cf. Jarrett et
al.2003, AJ, 125, 525). These values should be available for nearby normal
galaxies.

2. Alternatively, if the 2MASS K-band image of the target galaxy is available,
one may calculate the magnitude with m=m0-2.5*log(f), where m0 is the zero point
calibration magnitude (which may differ from image to image) and f is the total
flux of interest (accumulated from the D25 ellipse, for example), in default
2MASS image units, i.e., corresponding to the pixel value. Refer to
http://irsa.ipac.caltech.edu/applications/2MASS/LGA/calib.html for more details.

3. Look up the K-band extinction correction A_K from NED.

4. Calculate the luminosity: L_K=10^(-0.4*(m-A_K-M_sun))*D^2 (in units of 10^10
L_sun), where M_sun=3.41 is the K-band Solar absolute magnitude, D is the
distance of the target in units of Mpc, L_sun=5.67*10^31 erg/s is the K-band
Solar luminosity.

5. Convert L_K to M_K: M_K (in units of 10^10 M_sun) = L_K (in units of 10^10
L_sun) * 10^(-0.692+0.652*B_V), whhere B_V is the intrinsic B-V color of the
target galaxy, which can be found from NED. This empirical relation is obtained
by Bell & de Jong 2001, ApJ, 550, 212
